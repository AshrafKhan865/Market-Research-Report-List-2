<p><p>Rubble recycling involves various market types focused on the processing of construction debris. Crushing refers to the mechanical breakdown of rubble into smaller, reusable materials, facilitating easy transportation and application in new projects. Screening is the process of separating crushed materials based on size and quality, ensuring that only suitable aggregates are utilized. Stockpiling involves the temporary storage of recycled materials, allowing for organized management and accessibility, which is essential for efficient project execution and resource optimization.</p></p>
