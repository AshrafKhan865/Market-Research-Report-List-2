<p><p>The wood veneers market includes several types, each serving unique applications. Raw veneer is the natural, unprocessed form, often used in high-end woodworking. Paper-backed veneer features a paper layer for flexibility, making it suitable for curved surfaces. Phenolic-backed veneer has a durable backing for enhanced moisture resistance, ideal for commercial uses. Laid-up veneer is pre-assembled sheets, which offer ease in flooring and panels, while reconstituted veneer utilizes engineered wood fibers, providing a sustainable, consistent aesthetic for modern designs.</p></p>
