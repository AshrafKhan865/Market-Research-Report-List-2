<p><p>The Blood Meal market is categorized into several types based on the drying method used. Spray dried blood meal involves atomizing blood into fine droplets and rapidly drying with hot air, producing a fine powder. Air dried blood meal is made by exposing blood to ambient air, resulting in a coarse texture. Steam dried blood meal uses steam for quick evaporation, ensuring nutrient retention. Other methods may include freeze drying or drum drying, each offering unique properties for various applications in agriculture and animal feed.</p></p>
